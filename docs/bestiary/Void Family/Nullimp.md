📖 II. Lore

Nullimp is a tiny void‑sprite formed not from matter or code, but from the absence of both. It appears where reality thins — in the gaps between Nodes, in the shadows of broken geometry, or in the silent pockets of the Void Sector where even corruption refuses to linger.

Its body flickers like a missing frame, sometimes visible, sometimes not. Archivists describe Nullimps as “the shape of a thought that never happened.” They’re curious in a distant, alien way — observing the world as if they’re not entirely convinced it exists.

Nullimps don’t walk or fly. They simply are, and then aren’t, and then are again somewhere else.

Weaknesses:

Absence Blink — user vanishes for a moment, gaining guaranteed evasion for one hit

Void Dip — briefly phases out, lowering enemy accuracy

Flicker Step — rapid repositioning

Null Peck — tiny void‑strike that ignores some defenses

Nullimp excels at not being where the enemy expects it to be.

💛 V. Bonding Notes

Nullimp bonds through presence, ironically.It responds to players who remain calm, patient, and grounded — anchors in a world it doesn’t fully understand.

Once bonded, its flickering becomes more rhythmic around the player.
